Unlike acute heart failure, chronic heart failure is characterized by a gradual decline in cardiac function, often resulting from conditions such as coronary artery disease, hypertension, or previous heart attacks. Patients with CHF experience symptoms like fatigue, shortness of breath, and fluid retention, which can significantly impair their quality of life. Treatment for chronic heart failure primarily involves managing the underlying conditions, improving heart function, and preventing hospitalizations.
